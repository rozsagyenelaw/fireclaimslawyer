Estate Planning FAQs for Fire Victims

Should I wait until after my settlement to do estate planning?

No! Planning before you receive large settlements allows for better tax planning and asset protection. We can update your plan as your situation evolves.

I lost all my documents in the fire. Can you help?

Absolutely. With 25+ years of experience, I regularly help fire victims recreate and improve their estate plans. We'll obtain necessary records and create new, better documents.

How does estate planning protect my fire settlement?

Proper trusts can protect settlements from creditors, lawsuits, and predators while minimizing taxes. This ensures your recovery money actually helps you rebuild.

What if I'm still in litigation with insurance or utilities?

We plan for expected recoveries and structure your estate to maximize protection when settlements arrive. Early planning provides the most options.

Do I need estate planning if I don't have much left?

Yes! You're likely to receive settlements, and everyone needs healthcare directives and powers of attorney. Plus, our fire victim discounts make it affordable.
