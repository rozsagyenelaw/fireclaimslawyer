Insurance Claim FAQs

Answers to Common Questions

Do I really need an attorney for my insurance claim? ▼

While not required, statistics show policyholders with attorneys receive 40% more on average. Insurance companies have teams of lawyers and adjusters working to minimize payouts. We level the playing field and ensure you get every dollar you're entitled to. Our fee only comes from the additional money we recover.

My insurance company already made an offer. Is it too late? ▼

It's not too late! Initial offers rarely include everything you're owed. We can file supplemental claims for missed items, challenge undervaluations, and pursue additional coverages. Many clients come to us after receiving lowball offers and we significantly increase their recovery.

How much do you charge? ▼

We work on contingency - no upfront costs. We only get paid if we increase your recovery. Our fee is a percentage of the additional money we obtain above what the insurance company initially offers. If we don't increase your settlement, you owe us nothing.

What if my claim was denied? ▼

Don't give up! Many denied claims are wrongfully rejected. We can appeal the denial, provide additional documentation, and if necessary, file a bad faith lawsuit. Insurance companies often reverse denials when an attorney gets involved. Let us review your denial letter for free.

How long does the process take? ▼

Timeline varies by case complexity. Simple claims may resolve in 2-3 months. Complex total loss claims can take 6-12 months. Bad faith litigation may take longer but can result in significant additional compensation. We work to resolve claims as quickly as possible while maximizing your recovery.
